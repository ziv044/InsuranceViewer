# Pension Tax Benefits in Israel

## Employee Tax Benefits

### Tax Credit (Zikui Mas)
- 35% tax credit on employee pension contributions
- Up to ceiling of ~7,700 NIS/year (verify annually)
- Credit directly reduces tax liability
- Example: 6% of 20,000 NIS = 1,200/month * 35% = 420 NIS/month tax reduction

### Tax Deduction (Nikui Mas)
- Employer's pension contribution is not taxed as income for the employee
- Up to 7.5% of salary (combined pension + severance)
- Reduces taxable income, not tax directly
- Benefit depends on marginal tax rate

## Self-Employed Tax Benefits

### Pension Contributions
- First 4.14% of qualifying income: Tax deduction (reduces taxable income)
- Additional amounts up to 12.76%: Tax credit at 35%
- Qualifying income ceiling: ~20,520 NIS/month (verify)

### Keren Hishtalmut
- Deductible up to ~20,520 NIS/year
- Gains tax-free after 6 years
- One of the best tax benefits for self-employed

## Universal Benefits

### Keren Hishtalmut Gains
- After 6-year lock-in: ALL gains are tax-free
- No capital gains tax on withdrawal
- This is unique to Israeli tax system
- For employees: both employee and employer portions

### Pension Payout
- Monthly pension partially tax-exempt
- Exempt amount based on years of contribution and age
- Complex formula (consult pension advisor)

## Section 14 (Severance Arrangement)
- Employer ongoing pension contributions count toward severance obligation
- Employee keeps accumulated pension even if terminated
- Employer released from additional severance payment
- Most common arrangement in modern employment contracts

## Tax Optimization Tips
1. Maximize keren hishtalmut contributions (best tax-free vehicle)
2. Ensure pension contributions reach tax credit ceiling
3. Self-employed: maximize deductible pension contributions
4. Review management fees (even 0.5% difference compounds significantly)
5. Consider voluntary additional pension contributions for tax benefits
