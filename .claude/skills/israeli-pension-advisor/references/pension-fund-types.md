# Israeli Pension Fund Types

## Keren Pensia (Pension Fund)
- **Type:** Defined contribution (since 1995 reform)
- **Components:** Retirement savings + disability insurance + survivors insurance
- **Management fees:** 0.2-0.5% of accumulated balance + up to 1.05% of contributions
- **Investment:** Balanced portfolio managed by fund
- **Payout:** Monthly pension at retirement (annuity)
- **Major funds:** Menora Mivtachim, Migdal Makefet, Harel, Clal, Phoenix

### Advantages
- Lower management fees
- Built-in insurance coverage
- Guaranteed minimum return mechanism
- Simpler structure

### Disadvantages
- Less investment flexibility
- Cannot choose specific investment tracks (limited options)
- Monthly pension only (no lump sum option for post-2008 savings)

## Bituach Menahalim (Manager's Insurance)
- **Type:** Insurance-based savings product
- **Components:** Savings + separate risk insurance (disability, life)
- **Management fees:** 0.5-1.5% of balance + up to 4% of contributions
- **Investment:** Multiple tracks available (stocks, bonds, etc.)
- **Payout:** Monthly pension or partial lump sum

### Advantages
- More investment track options
- Potential for partial lump sum withdrawal
- Better for high earners wanting control
- Separate insurance component

### Disadvantages
- Higher management fees
- Separate risk premium reduces savings
- More complex structure

## Keren Hishtalmut (Training Fund)
- **Type:** Medium-term savings
- **Lock-in:** 6 years (3 years for education/training)
- **Tax:** Gains tax-free after 6 years (for employees)
- **Contribution ceiling for tax benefit:** ~15,712 NIS/year employee (2.5% of ceiling salary)
- **Most popular savings product in Israel**

### For Employees
- Employee: 2.5% of salary
- Employer: 7.5% of salary
- Tax-free ceiling: employer contribution up to 7.5% of ~26,000 NIS/month

### For Self-Employed
- Maximum deductible: ~20,520 NIS/year
- Tax benefit: deduction from taxable income

## Kupat Gemel (Provident Fund)
- General savings/investment vehicle
- Less common since 2008 pension reforms
- More flexible withdrawal rules
- Capital gains tax applies
